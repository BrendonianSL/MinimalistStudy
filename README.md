# MinimalistStudy
Minimalist web design is a style of web design that emphasizes simplicity. The antithesis to maximalist, it strives to keep things clean, orderly, and not cluttered; Taking advantage of whitespace to sell a spacious design. Here I will lay out what I found in my Minimalist Design study to talk about why this form of web development appeals to so many.

## 1: Dominant use of Sans-Serif fonts/typefaces
When studying what sells the minimalist feel, nothing matches more of a minimalist style than that of a simple typeface. Some sites may mix and match different kinds of fonts, but Sans-Serif or typefaces akin to that are also in use. Some sites even go so far as to use the same with different weights to sell the feel. Typefaces such as [Inter](https://fonts.google.com/specimen/Inter) and [Roboto](https://fonts.google.com/specimen/Roboto) are very common amongst these webpages.

## Use of neutral colors
Neutral colors are predominantly used when viewing designs that boast a minimalist feel. These color choices make it easier to establish hierarchy within a design and generally convey of feeling of cleanliness. Neutral colors are also in light and darkmode pages making the color scheme easy to work with. If web pages do have colors, they only ever stray away from neutrals to use a color as a CTA (Call To Action) to draw user attention.

## Simple Iconography
On the websites that have it, they use icongraphy that doesn't have a lot of details and thinner lines. These help continute sell the simplistic feel. However, using them sparsley is important and only use them when necessary.

## Thin lines
Thinner lines are common on minimalist websites. Sometimes they may even be dotted.

## White Space
Minimalist sites use white space to their advantage to make the site feel spacious and clean. It also helps focus the user on certain important section of a site.

## Rounded images/cards
Rounded images and cards are commonly used to sell a more friendly appeal to these sites. Doing so can make your site feel more approachable than normal.

## Unique Stylistic Choices
Because of the lack of eye catching design on these sites, designers don't have much free room to create eye catching or highly stylized elements into their sites. They seek to add more personality to these sites by having textured backgrounds, numbered sections, microinteractions, or moving animations!

# Turning Research Into A Design.
So the goal of this project is to create a project with the following requirements.

- Leverage white space to create a clear and concise design.
- Implement rounded cards/images when applicable.
- Implement iconography where necessary.
- Limit color choices to whites, blacks, grays, and ONE call to action color when necessary.
- Leveraging ONE sans serif typeface with different weights/shades to develop hierarchy.
